ForestNet: Deforestation Driver Classification in Indonesia

Presented at NeurIPS 2020 workshop on Tackling Climate Change with Machine Learning. Full paper in progress.

I'm currently working in the Stanford ML Group on classifying what is driving forest loss in Indonesia, using satellite imagery. I was a part of the AI for Climate Change bootcamp, and I've continued to work on this project since graduating from the bootcamp in March 2020.


SimGrade: Using Code Similarity Measures for More Accurate Human Grading

Presented as a poster paper at Educational Data Mining 2021. Full paper in progress.

Anyone who has TA'd a CS class knows that exam grading is the most difficult part of the job. There's a significant amount of inconsistency and error that results from human grading. If a grader looks at a particular student's submission and has recently seen something similar, she will be able to grade the current submission more accurately. With this insight, I led a team that proposed several algorithms to improve human grading.
