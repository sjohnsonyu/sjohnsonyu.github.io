Music

I'm a classically trained vocalist and a singer/songwriter. I started playing piano at the age of five and viola at the age of nine, started choir at age ten, and haven't stopped singing since. I'm a member of the group Stanford Talisman. Talisman is a singing group founded in 1990 to share music that was historically unheard. A large portion of our repertoire is derived from the struggle songs of the anti-Apartheid movement in South Africa, but the range of our music spans the globe.

The creation of music has always enraptured me. I find it miraculous that we can create worlds that didn't exist before through the writing of a single song. I try to capture this sense of wonder with the world in my music. I also believe that music is meant to move, to create change, spark thought, or promote healing, and I aspire to move through my music.

My senior project in high school was writing the music for a one-act musical Always Have, Always Will, a show about long-distance relationships, opening up, and letting go. My co-writer, Emma Radich, and I spent two and half weeks doing nothing but writing and culminated the project with a staged reading. To this day, creating a full-length musical remains one of my life ambitions.
