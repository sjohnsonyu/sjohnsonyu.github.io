Music

My music major is in choral conducting, but I'm also a student in voice. I started playing piano at the age of five and viola at the age of nine, started choir at age ten, and haven't stopped singing since. I sing Soprano 1 in the Stanford Chamber Chorale and had the privilege of guest conducting Eleanor Daley's In Remembrance during our tour to Thailand.

During the 2017-2018 school year, my big project was directing the singing group Stanford Talisman. Talisman is a singing group founded in 1990 to share music that carries compelling personal and cultural narratives. A large portion of our repertoire is derived from songs of the anti-Apartheid movement in South Africa, but the range of our music spans the globe. As Director, I led us to delve deeper into educating ourselves about the historical roots of the music we sing, so that we could continually improve our ability as storytellers and grow as thinkers.

I've dabbled in composition and songwriting. I wrote the music for the one-act musical Always Have, Always Will, a show about long-distance relationships, opening up, and letting go. Music is meant to move, to create change, spark thought, or promote healing, and I aspire to move through my music.
