About

Hello! I'm a first-year computer science PhD candidate at Harvard, advised by Milind Tambe. I recently wrapped up my master's in computer science (concentration in Artificial Intelligence) at Stanford. My undergrad was a double major in CS and music (concentration in conducting). I love computer science for its empowering potential to create socially impactful solutions, and I'm passionate about music as a singer/conductor and also from a musicological perspective. When I'm not coding or practicing, you'll likely find me outdoors - trail running, bouldering, adventuring, or journaling.

I love working on hard problems that have positive social impact. During my master's, I was a part of the Stanford ML Group (headed by Andrew Ng), working on a project about predicting direct drivers of deforestation in Indonesia. I've also conducted research in computational education (CompEdu Lab), proposing ways to improve human grading.

For me, data are full of wonder. One of the greatest joys in life is a clean, well-organized dataset ready to be analyzed!

As I strive to be lifelong learner, an essential part of my learning is teaching. I've taught computer science for five years, TA'd in the music department, and tutored in a variety of subjects, including music theory, algebra, and Latin. Helping others grow alongside me is my passion.

I love the thrill of a working on a project, whether it's CS, music, organizing, or art. If you have any ideas that you want to bring to life with me, feel free to reach out!
